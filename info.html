I am a [typographic/interaction/product] designer in San Francisco.

I am the Principal Designer at Twitter where I lead the design of key initiatives across the Twitter product. I am also an Advisor/Mentor at The Designer Fund, whose mission is to invest in designers who create businesses with positive social impact. I am the co-creator 52 Weeks of UX, an online resource created to provide “discourse on the process of designing for real people.”

I also have a serious infatuation with typography—especially web typography—and created a couple little tools that hopefully make the world a better place, typographically speaking. I built Ffffallback to help people create bulletproff font-stacks and Responsive Measure as a way to connect responsive designs to an ideal measure.

I have been incredibly blessed to work with amazing people on some amazing products. This year, Fast Company named me one of 50 Designers shaping the future of Design, which I consider a win for designers everywhere who roll up their sleeves, do the work, and sweat the details.
